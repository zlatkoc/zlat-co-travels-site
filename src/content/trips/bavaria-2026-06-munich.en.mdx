<ReadingBlock dropcap place="Munich" coord="48.135° N, 11.582° E" meta="June 24–26 — the excuse">
The reason for being here fits in one sentence: two days of the
[Event Modeling Conference](https://www.nebulit.de/eventmodeling-conference-2026), June 25th and
26th. That was the plan, and the plan held. Everything else in this story is what happened around
it.

What no plan accounted for was the heat. Late June 2026 turned into a record European heatwave —
Germany broke its all-time temperature record twice in one weekend, 41.3°C on Friday and 41.5°C on
Saturday — and Munich spent the week around 35°C with no intention of cooling down after dark.
</ReadingBlock>

<ReadingBlock place="Eisbach" coord="48.143° N, 11.588° E" meta="Munich's answer to the heat">
Munich's answer to the heat is the river. People were dipping into the Isar everywhere, and the
English Garden looked like one long lido — every stream lined with towels, every current carrying a
slow parade of heads downstream. And at the Eisbach wave, the surfers queued in wetsuits as if the
Alps had shipped them a piece of ocean.

<ReadingBlock place="Löwenbräukeller" coord="48.147° N, 11.549° E" meta="The match">
One evening belonged to the World Cup: Germany against Ecuador on the big screen in the
Löwenbräukeller Biergarten. It was too hot for proper beer, so I did the only sensible thing a
guest can do in a Bavarian beer garden — ordered a Radler and didn't apologise for it. Germany led
after two minutes and still managed to lose 2–1, which the garden took with remarkable,
well-watered composure.

<ReadingBlock place="Munich by bike" coord="48.135° N, 11.582° E" meta="Two wheels through the heat">
The best way to see Munich that week was from a saddle. By bike the heat became airflow, and the
city unfolded at exactly the right speed — out along the Isar, through the English Garden, up to
the Olympiapark and back. On the long straight avenues north of the city you could ride for
minutes without touching the brakes, past a steady procession of other cyclists with the same
idea.
</ReadingBlock>
